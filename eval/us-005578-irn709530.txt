Otto Gabriel Gara (1925-2010) was born on 31 July 1925 in Budapest, Hungary to Julius Gara (1891-1944) and Aranka Fleischer. His parents married in Budapest in 1924. He survived several labor camps in Hungary, and later lived in a safe house supported by Swedish diplomat Raoul Wallenberg. His father Julius was imprisoned 20 March 1944 in Kistarcsa concentration camp. He was later deported to Auschwitz where he perished. After the war, he studied law at Eötvös Loránd University. Otto married Agnes Benedek in 1952. The couple immigrated to the United States in 1956, the same year as the Hungarian Revolution of 1956. They lived in several cities, including Los Angeles and Chicago. Otto worked as a librarian. They had one son, Steven.

Agnes Gara (1932-2013) was born Agnes Ann Benedek on 20 July 1932 in Budapest, Hungary to Hermann and Erzsebet Benedek. She survived the Holocaust in Budapest in a safe house supported by Swedish diplomat Raoul Wallenberg. Her father Hermann was arrested on 16 November 1944 and deported to Buchenwald on 19 November where he likely perished. After the war, Agnes studied science at Eötvös Loránd University. She married Otto Gara in 1952. The couple immigrated to the United States in 1956, the same year as the Hungarian Revolution of 1956. They lived in several cities, including Los Angeles and Chicago. Agnes worked as a scientist. They had one son, Steven.

The collection documents the Holocaust-era experiences of Otto and Agnes Gara, both of whom survived the Holocaust in Budapest, Hungary. Included are school papers, identification cards, immigration papers, and photographs.
