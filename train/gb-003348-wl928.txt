Kobylinski family: correspondence during internment

1 folder

The collection was deposited by their daughter, Hanna Kobylinski Rozental in 1990, having been discovered amongst the possessions of her late sister, Eva Samson, in London.

See 928/87 for more detailed biographical notes on the family. The subject of the collection is mainly family business although a sense of the troubled times is ever present. The correspondence is written in both English and German

This collection consists of correspondence between Else and Sigismund Kobylinski, German Jewish refugees, during their internment on the Isle of Man in Summer and Autumn 1940. The correspondents came to Great Britain in 1939, their children having emigrated some years earlier.
