Records of the New York office of the American Jewish Joint Distribution Committee, 1945-1954.

2510 files.

This fonds is of crucial importance for the history of the reconstruction of the Jewish community in Belgium after the Second World War. In Subcollection Countries & Regions, Record Group: Belgium we firstly note the files (containing correspondence, memos, reports etc.) on the financial administration of JDC aid, fundraising and the transfer of funds to Belgium; see the files nrs. 152, 153, 154, 155 and 160 for the period 1945-1954. Nr. 149 (years 1944-1954) contains general correspondence and reports on JDC aid to Belgium, the treatment and legal status of Jewish refugees in Belgium, … The same subcollection also contains files, usually holding correspondence and reports, on the following subjects: Jewish children hidden by the AJB (nr. 150; years 1944-1954), medical needs (nr. 157; 1947-1954), “[…]Loan Kassas, Caisse de Prêts et de Crédit Brussels; Joodsche Cooperative Leenkas, Antwerp” (nr. 156; 1945-1950), relief supplies (nr. 161, 1945-1949), refugees (nr. 158; 1945-1948) and returnees (nr. 159; 1954). There are files (containing correspondence, statistics, publications, reports, …) on the following organisations and institutions: AIVG (nrs. 163-164; years 1945-1958), École Israélite and Yeshiva Etz Hayim (nr. 165; 1947-1954), Jesode-Hatora-Beth-Jacob (nr. 166; 1946-1954), Yeshiva Kehillat Yaakov (nr. 167; 1947-1950) and other schools and educational institutions (nr. 151; 1945-1950). A number of files in other subcollections/subsections also concern the Belgian Jewish community. Belgium is explicitly mentioned in the following files: “Memoranda” (nr. 2121; year 1946), “Cultural Committee meetings” (nr. 2163, 2165-2166, 2169; years 1947, 1949, 1950, 1953), “Medical Advisory Committee, general and meetings” (nr. 2193; 1945-1954), “Administration Committee meetings” (nr. 2141-2142; 1945), “Argentina, financial” (nr. 885; 1945-1949), “Germany, displaced persons” (nr. 307; 1951),”Germany, DP’s, returnees” (nr. 332; 1950-1954), “Germany, emigration” (nr. 341; 1946-1948), “Germany, emigration: yeshiva students” (nr. 342; 1947-1948), “Germany, relief supplies” (nr. 356; 1944-1945), “Passover” (nr. 1302; 1949), “US Office of the Foreign Liquidation Commissioner” (nrs. 2038-2039; 1949-1951), “Belgian War Relief Society” (nr. 1514; 1946-1947), “Jewish Education Committee of New York” (nr. 1514; 1946-1947), “Mizrachi organisation” (nr. 1810; 1945-1949), “World Jewish Congress” (nrs. 2071-2072; 1946-1947), “Vaad Hatzala” (nrs. 2057-2058; 1947), “Children” (nr. 1029 and 1031; 1945-1946), “Conversion” (nr. 1035; 1945-1954), “Migration – promissory notes” (nr. 1146; 1950-1953) and “Migration – service account, Belgium” (nr. 1149; 1947-1949). The file “Belgium, survivors (individuals)” (nr. 162) contains brief accounts of individual survivors of internment, collected by the Service d’Évacuation et Regroupement in Brussels. Lists of survivors of “Camps and locations” in Belgium (dated 1945) can be found in folder nr. 1054. Finally, the folders entiteld “Rescue Children” (nrs. 1896-1897; years 1946-1949, 1954, 1962) contain correspondence, reports and memos on the responsibility for children in the program “Rescue Children”, i.a. in homes in Belgium.
